## ğŸŒŸ About This Work

### Significance
One of the most influential language learning texts for British colonial administrators in 19th-century India.

### Contents
- **Grammar**: Systematic presentation of Hindustani structures
- **Vocabulary**: Bilingual English-Hindustani word lists
- **Dialogues**: Practical conversational exercises
- **Stories**: Reading practice in both Persian and Nagari scripts
- **Military Terms**: Specialized vocabulary for military commands
- **Script Instruction**: Both Persian (Nastaliq) and Nagari (Devanagari)

### Historical Impact
- Shaped how generations of British officials learned Hindustani
- Influenced British perceptions of Indian languages
- Documents mid-19th century Hindustani usage
- Critical period when British codified "Hindustani" as administrative language

### Author Background
**John Shakespear (1774-1858)**
- East India Company Professor of Hindustani
- Also published: Hindustani Grammar (1813), Hindustani-English Dictionary (1817)
- Retired 1829 with pension
- Replaced Gilchrist's works as standard references
