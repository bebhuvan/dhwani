# The Imperial Gazetteer of India (Complete 9-Volume Set)

## Overview

The Imperial Gazetteer of India by William Wilson Hunter (1881) is a comprehensive geographical and statistical dictionary covering all territories under British Indian administration. This monumental 9-volume reference work provides detailed information on:

- **Geography**: Cities, towns, villages, districts, mountains, rivers, and physical features
- **Statistics**: Population census data, economic production, trade figures
- **Administration**: District boundaries, administrative divisions, civil stations
- **History**: Historical notes on major locations and regions
- **Ethnography**: Demographic information on communities and populations

Hunter's Gazetteer became the standard geographical reference for India in the late 19th century and was later expanded into the even more comprehensive Imperial Gazetteer of India (26 volumes, 1907-1909).

## Historical Significance

William Wilson Hunter (1840-1900) was a Scottish historian and civil servant who served the British Indian government for over 25 years. His Imperial Gazetteer represented the first comprehensive attempt to systematically document the geography, demographics, and administration of British India. The work drew on district gazetteers, census data, and administrative reports to create an authoritative reference that served officials, scholars, and the general public.
