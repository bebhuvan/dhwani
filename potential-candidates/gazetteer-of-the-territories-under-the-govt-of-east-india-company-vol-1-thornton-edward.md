# A Gazetteer of the Territories under the Government of the East-India Company (Complete 4-Volume Set)

## Overview

Edward Thornton's Gazetteer (1854) represents one of the most comprehensive geographical references for mid-19th century British India. Published across 4 volumes, this alphabetical dictionary documents thousands of locations across territories administered by the East India Company.

**Contents Include:**
- **Geography**: Precise locations, elevations, physical features
- **Demographics**: Population statistics, ethnic composition
- **Administration**: District boundaries, civil stations, revenue divisions
- **Economics**: Agriculture, trade, manufacturing, revenue data
- **History**: Historical notes on major locations and regions
- **Infrastructure**: Roads, communications, public buildings

## Historical Significance

**Edward Thornton** compiled this gazetteer during a critical period of East India Company expansion. The work synthesized information from:
- District surveys and settlement reports
- Revenue records and tax assessments
- Census data and demographic surveys
- Military reconnaissance reports
- Travelers' accounts and local histories

The Gazetteer served multiple purposes:
- **Administrative Tool**: Helped British officials govern vast territories
- **Economic Reference**: Aided commercial planning and resource allocation
- **Military Planning**: Provided strategic geographical information
- **Scholarly Resource**: Enabled academic study of Indian geography and society

This work preceded the more extensive Imperial Gazetteer of India (1881) by William Wilson Hunter and documents mid-century India before the 1857 rebellion and the transfer of power from the Company to the Crown.
