# The Vishnu Purana: A System of Hindu Mythology and Tradition (Complete 5-Volume Set)

## Overview

The Vishnu Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas, major texts in Hindu literature that narrate cosmology, mythology, and religious philosophy. This complete 5-volume translation by Horace Hayman Wilson (1864-1877) was groundbreaking as the first comprehensive English rendering of a major Purana.

The text covers:
- **Cosmology**: Creation and structure of the universe
- **Mythology**: Legends and incarnations (avatars) of Vishnu
- **Genealogies**: Lines of gods, sages, and kings
- **Geography**: Sacred places and cosmic regions
- **Philosophy**: Teachings on dharma, karma, and liberation
- **Yugas**: Cycles of time and world ages

## Historical Significance

**Translator**: Horace Hayman Wilson (1786-1860) was the first Boden Professor of Sanskrit at Oxford University and one of the pioneering Indologists. His translation work made Hindu scriptures accessible to Western scholarship.

**Editor**: Fitzedward Hall (1825-1901) edited the posthumous volumes after Wilson's death, maintaining scholarly standards while completing the project.

The Vishnu Purana translation influenced Victorian-era understanding of Hinduism and remains a valuable historical document showing early Western engagement with Sanskrit religious texts.
