# Priority Breakdown - Missing Gutenberg Works

## Tier 1: Essential Missing Works (10 works) - ADD THESE FIRST

### Buddhist Literature

#### 1. The Light of Asia (eBook #8920)
- **Author:** Sir Edwin Arnold
- **Category:** Buddhist Poetry/Epic
- **Priority:** CRITICAL
- **Why Essential:** One of the most influential English-language Buddhist texts ever written. This 1879 epic poem introduced millions of Westerners to Buddhism and the life of Buddha. A landmark work in cross-cultural religious literature.
- **Current Gap:** Repository has Indian Hindu epics but lacks major Buddhist literary classics.

#### 2. The Essence of Buddhism (eBook #18223)
- **Author:** Sir Edwin Arnold
- **Category:** Buddhist Philosophy
- **Priority:** CRITICAL
- **Why Essential:** Companion to "The Light of Asia" - provides accessible philosophical framework for Buddhist teachings.
- **Current Gap:** Need accessible Buddhist philosophy texts alongside Hindu philosophy.

### Sanskrit Scholarship & Biography

#### 3. Sacred Books of the East (eBook #12894)
- **Compiler:** F. Max Müller (editor)
- **Category:** Sacred Texts Anthology
- **Priority:** CRITICAL
- **Why Essential:** Monumental 19th-century collection including Vedic hymns, Upanishads, Dhammapada, and other Eastern sacred texts. Historical importance in bringing Eastern philosophy to Western readers.
- **Current Gap:** Individual texts are covered, but this anthology has historical/scholarly significance.

#### 4. Lectures on the Science of Language (eBook #32856)
- **Author:** F. Max Müller
- **Category:** Linguistics/Sanskrit Studies
- **Priority:** HIGH
- **Why Essential:** Foundational work in comparative philology and Sanskrit studies. Shows how Sanskrit scholarship developed in the West.
- **Current Gap:** Repository lacks works on Sanskrit linguistics and comparative philology.

#### 5. Auld lang syne: My Indian friends (eBook #73047)
- **Author:** F. Max Müller
- **Category:** Biography/India
- **Priority:** CRITICAL
- **Why Essential:** Personal accounts of Max Müller's relationships with Indian scholars who taught him Sanskrit and collaborated on translations. Provides human dimension to 19th-century Sanskrit scholarship.
- **Current Gap:** Need biographical/personal narratives about Indian-Western scholarly exchange.

#### 6. Auld lang syne (eBook #68517)
- **Author:** F. Max Müller
- **Category:** Autobiography
- **Priority:** HIGH
- **Why Essential:** Max Müller's autobiography - critical context for understanding how Sanskrit texts were translated and transmitted to the West.
- **Current Gap:** Biographical context for the translations already in the repository.

#### 7. My Autobiography: A Fragment (eBook #30269)
- **Author:** F. Max Müller
- **Category:** Autobiography
- **Priority:** HIGH
- **Why Essential:** Additional autobiographical material, particularly about undertaking the Rig Veda translation work.
- **Current Gap:** Scholar biographies provide context for the texts.

### Islamic-Indian Philosophy

#### 8. The Development of Metaphysics in Persia (eBook #41707)
- **Author:** Sir Muhammad Iqbal
- **Category:** Philosophy/Islamic Studies
- **Priority:** HIGH
- **Why Essential:** Complements Iqbal's "Secrets of the Self" already in repository. Important for understanding Islamic philosophy in Indian context.
- **Current Gap:** Repository has Iqbal's poetry but lacks his philosophical prose.

### Social Reform & Women's Rights

#### 9. Autobiographical Sketches (eBook #11376)
- **Author:** Annie Besant
- **Category:** Biography/Social Reform
- **Priority:** HIGH
- **Why Essential:** Besant was a major figure in Indian independence movement, women's rights, and education reform. Her autobiography provides crucial historical context.
- **Current Gap:** Need biographical works about Western advocates for Indian independence.

#### 10. Marriage: As It Was, As It Is, and As It Should Be (eBook #47000)
- **Author:** Annie Besant
- **Category:** Social Reform/Women's Rights
- **Priority:** HIGH
