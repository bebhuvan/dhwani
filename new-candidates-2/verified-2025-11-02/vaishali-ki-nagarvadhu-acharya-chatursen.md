# Vaishali ki Nagarvadhu

## Overview

First published in 1948, the novel dramatizes the life of Amrapali, famed courtesan of the Licchavi republic. Chatursen reconstructs the political intrigues of Vaishali, rivalries with Magadha, and the socio-religious ferment preceding the Buddha’s ministry. Through Amrapali’s journey—from court entertainer to Buddhist disciple—the novel explores themes of love, power, renunciation, and the transformation of republican ideals under imperial pressure.

## Features

- Rich historical detail drawn from Pali canon, Buddhist chronicles, and archaeological research.
- Depiction of republican governance, assembly debates, and diplomatic missions.
- Interweaving of philosophical dialogues between Amrapali, Ajatashatru, the Buddha, and Confucian travellers.

## Legacy

The novel became a landmark of Hindi historical fiction, inspiring stage and film adaptations and cementing Amrapali as a cultural icon. It is studied for its blend of meticulous research and dramatic storytelling.

## Public Domain Status

Acharya Chatursen died in 1960; the novel entered the Indian public domain on 1 January 2021. It is also in the public domain in the United States (pre-1964 publication without renewal).
