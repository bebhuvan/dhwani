# Coins, Medals, and Seals, Ancient and Modern

## Overview

American journalist William Cowper Prime offers lay readers a sweeping introduction to coinage and medallic art. The book narrates the evolution of money from ancient Greek and Roman issues to Islamic, Indian, and medieval European series. It also treats commemorative medals and the use of seals in heraldry and administration. Numerous engravings reproduce notable specimens from museum and private collections, while appendices discuss preservation techniques and reference works for collectors.

## Significance

- Among the earliest English-language popular guides to numismatics, promoting the systematic collection and study of coins in the United States.
- Provides cross-cultural comparisons that remain useful to historians tracing monetary iconography and imperial propaganda.
- Includes references to early catalogues and hoard discoveries, documenting the state of numismatic scholarship in the mid-nineteenth century.

## Public Domain Status

Published in 1861, the book is in the public domain worldwide.
