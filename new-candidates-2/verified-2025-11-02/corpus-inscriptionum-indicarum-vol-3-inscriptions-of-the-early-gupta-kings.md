# Corpus Inscriptionum Indicarum, Volume III: Inscriptions of the Early Gupta Kings

## Overview

Fleet’s volume assembles the Sanskrit inscriptions of the Gupta dynasty (4th–6th century CE), including the Allahabad Pillar prashasti, Mathura and Eran inscriptions, and copper-plate charters. Texts are printed in Nagari and transliterated into Roman script, accompanied by English translations and extensive notes on genealogy, chronology, and administrative terminology.

## Significance

- Essential for reconstructing Gupta political history, art patronage, and the development of classical Sanskrit prose.
- Provides high-quality facsimiles and estampages indispensable for palaeographic analysis.
- Informs comparative studies of royal panegyrics across South and Southeast Asia.

## Public Domain Status

Published in 1888, the volume is in the public domain worldwide.
