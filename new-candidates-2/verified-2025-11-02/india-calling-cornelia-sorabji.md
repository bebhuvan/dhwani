# India Calling

## Overview

Sorabji narrates her pioneering journey from Poona to Oxford and back to colonial courts, detailing the challenges she faced as a woman barrister and social reformer. She chronicles cases involving secluded purdahnashin women, debates on child marriage, legal education reforms, and interactions with nationalist leaders.

## Themes

- Gender barriers in the legal profession and the sociocultural constraints on elite women.
- Reflections on nationalism, imperial governance, and the need for gradual reform.
- Vivid portraits of clients, colleagues, and Indian princes, revealing the complexities of early twentieth-century society.

## Legacy

The memoir is a key primary source for gender and legal history in South Asia, illuminating the experiences of the first Indian woman to practice law. It complements Sorabji’s earlier autobiographical writings (*Between the Twilights*).

## Public Domain Status

Cornelia Sorabji died in 1954; *India Calling* entered the Indian public domain on 1 January 2015. It is also public domain in the United States (pre-1964 with no renewal).
