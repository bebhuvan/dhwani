# Catalogue of the Coins in the Indian Museum, Calcutta (Volume I)

## Overview

Volume I covers the Indian Museum’s holdings from punch-marked coins through the Gupta period. Each entry records metal, weight, size, symbols, provenance, and bibliographic references. Plates reproduce detailed photographs, aiding iconographic study.

## Highlights

- Provides pioneering typologies for punch-marked series, Mauryan imperial issues, Sunga bronzes, and Satavahana portrait coins.
- Includes sections on tribal and regional issues (e.g., Audumbara, Kuninda) with epigraphic readings.
- Offers historical essays linking coinage to dynastic events and trade routes.

## Research Value

This catalogue remains a primary source for Indian numismatics, giving provenance details for pieces now dispersed. Scholars reference its numbering systems and plates in modern catalogues.

## Public Domain Status

Published in 1906, the volume is in the public domain worldwide.
