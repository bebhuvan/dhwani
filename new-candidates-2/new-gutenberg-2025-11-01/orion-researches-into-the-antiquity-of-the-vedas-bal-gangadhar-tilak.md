# Orion; or, Researches into the Antiquity of the Vedas

## Overview

Published in Pune in 1893, *Orion* precedes Tilak’s *Arctic Home* and lays the groundwork for dating Vedic hymns via astronomical clues. Tilak interprets references to the constellation Orion (*Mrigaśiras*) to place the composition of certain hymns several millennia BCE.

## Structure and Themes

The book surveys Vedic passages mentioning stars, equinoxes, and solstices, combining them with calculations about the precession of the equinoxes. Tilak argues that precise celestial observations imply a sophisticated priesthood and suggests that Vedic religion developed in stages aligned with astronomical shifts. The study blends textual exegesis with nationalist pride in ancient scientific knowledge.

## Historical and Cultural Significance

*Orion* attracted attention from Orientalists and Indian reformers alike, challenging colonial chronologies that marginalised Vedic antiquity. It contributed to the emergence of “scientific” Hindu nationalism and encouraged subsequent scholars to engage astronomy in historical inquiry—even when disagreeing with Tilak’s conclusions.

## Public Domain Status

As a work published in 1893 by Tilak (d. 1920), *Orion* is firmly in the public domain. The Internet Archive hosts scans of early editions for philological study.
