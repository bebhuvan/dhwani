The Upanishads

Multiple Authors
Language Sanskrit
Period 800–200 BCE
Genre Philosophy, Spirituality
Read Full Text

Ancient philosophical texts that form the theoretical foundation of Hindu thought. The Upanishads explore fundamental questions about the nature of reality, consciousness, the self, and liberation. Composed over several centuries, these texts represent some of humanity's earliest systematic philosophical inquiry.

The word "Upanishad" means "sitting down near"—referring to students sitting at the feet of a teacher. These dialogues between sages and seekers continue to influence philosophical thought worldwide.

Gitanjali

Rabindranath Tagore
Language Bengali, English
Year 1910
Genre Poetry
Read Full Text

A collection of 103 poems that won Rabindranath Tagore the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913, making him the first non-European to receive the honor. The title means "Song Offerings" in Bengali.

Tagore translated his own Bengali verses into English prose poems, creating a bridge between Eastern spiritual poetry and Western literary traditions. The collection explores themes of devotion, nature, and the divine with remarkable lyrical grace.
